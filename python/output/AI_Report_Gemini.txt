# üìà Comprehensive Business Intelligence Report: E-commerce Marketplace Performance

## 1. Introduction

This report provides a comprehensive interpretation and synthesis of pre-computed analytical results for an e-commerce marketplace. The primary objective is to extract meaningful business insights from financial, customer, product, operational, and seller performance data. It aims to identify key trends, highlight opportunities and risks, explain underlying patterns, and propose actionable recommendations to enhance business strategy and operational efficiency.

## 2. Data Quality Analysis

The analytical foundation of this report is built upon 8 distinct datasets, totaling over 1.5 million rows and 48 columns. A thorough data quality assessment was conducted at the source level, revealing several points for upstream consideration:

*   **Duplication Issues**: The `GEOLOCATION` dataset exhibited a significant number of duplicated rows (261,831, representing 26.18% of its total). While these duplicates were handled during the data preparation phase, their presence at the source indicates potential inefficiencies in data collection or storage for geographic information.
*   **Missing Values**: The `ORDERS` table contained null values in 3 out of 8 columns, and the `PRODUCTS` table showed nulls in 8 out of 9 columns. Although the total null values were below a critical threshold and the data completeness score was 77.08%, these missing attributes in product and order data could limit richer analyses if not addressed at the source.
*   **Low Cardinality**: The `ORDERS` and `ORDER_PAYMENTS` tables each had one column with low cardinality (e.g., `order_status`, `payment_type`), which is not necessarily an issue but is noted for data structure.

**Important Note**: It is crucial to emphasize that all analytical models and results presented in this report are based on a meticulously cleaned, deduplicated, and preprocessed dataset. The identified data quality issues pertain to the raw source data and do not compromise the integrity or accuracy of the derived insights. They serve primarily to inform future data governance efforts and improvements in upstream data collection systems.

## 3. Data-Driven Insights

### 3.1. üìä Executive Summary: Key Performance Overview & Strategic Implications

The marketplace demonstrates a substantial operational scale with **$15.42 million in total revenue** across **96,477 orders** from **93,357 unique customers** and supported by **2,970 sellers**.

**Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):**
*   **Average Order Value (AOV):** $159.86
*   **Average Basket Size:** 1.14 items
*   **Revenue per Customer:** $165.20
*   **Orders per Customer:** 1.03
*   **Revenue per Seller:** $5,192.75
*   **Orders per Seller:** 32.48

These metrics suggest a high average transaction value but a predominantly single-purchase customer base, as indicated by the average orders per customer being just 1.03. This implies a heavy reliance on new customer acquisition rather than repeat business. The revenue and order contribution per seller are relatively low, suggesting a long tail of smaller sellers and potential opportunities for seller development or consolidation.

### 3.2. üìà Financial Performance: Revenue & Growth Trajectory

The marketplace experienced a period of growth from October 2016 to August 2018. The average monthly revenue over the entire 22-month analysis period was $701,020.99, but this increased to **$991,834.39 in the last 12 months**, demonstrating positive momentum.

**Key Trends and Seasonality:**
*   **November Peak**: November 2017 saw a remarkable **53.57% Month-over-Month (MoM) revenue growth** and a 62.77% MoM increase in orders, reaching **$1,153,528.05**. This significant surge aligns with Black Friday and holiday shopping seasons, confirmed by daily sales anomalies on November 24th and 25th, 2017.
*   **Post-Peak Dip**: Following the November peak, December 2017 experienced a **-26.90% MoM revenue decline**, which is a typical post-holiday season adjustment.
*   **Early 2018 Rebound**: January 2018 showed strong recovery with a **27.92% MoM revenue growth**, surpassing the previous peak in orders.
*   **Recent Stagnation**: Towards the end of the analysis period (April to August 2018), monthly revenue growth rates became volatile, with small gains (April, July) or declines (May, June, August), suggesting a potential plateau or slowdown in growth requiring attention.

### 3.3. üë• Customer Insights: Engagement & Value

Customer analysis highlights critical areas for improving long-term value.

**RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) Segmentation (Last 12 Months):**
*   **Potential Loyalists (48.12% customers, 57.16% revenue)** and **Loyal Customers (12.12% customers, 24.53% revenue)** together represent the core revenue drivers. This large base of loyal and potentially loyal customers indicates a strong existing customer foundation.
*   **At Risk (35.75% customers, 17.30% revenue)** is a substantial segment that requires proactive re-engagement strategies to prevent churn.
*   **Lost (3.99% customers, 0.96% revenue)** represents a smaller, yet valuable, segment where win-back campaigns could be considered.
*   **Champions (0.01% customers, 0.04% revenue)** are a very small, high-value segment, indicating highly engaged and profitable customers.

**Cohort Analysis (2017-11 to 2018-09):**
*   **Critically Low Retention**: The cohort analysis reveals extremely low customer retention rates. After the initial purchase month (Month 0, 100% retention), retention dramatically drops to **0.41% by Month 1**, further declining to **0.14% by Month 9**.
*   **Zero Repeat Purchase Revenue**: A highly concerning finding is the **$0.00 average revenue** reported for active customers in all subsequent months (Month 1 to Month 9). This implies that while a tiny fraction of customers might technically "retain" (e.g., log in), they are not making any significant repeat purchases that translate into revenue. This contradicts the concept of "Loyal Customers" contributing to ongoing revenue, suggesting that the marketplace is heavily transactional and struggles to generate value from repeat business.

**Implications**: The business is highly dependent on continuous new customer acquisition. Customer Lifetime Value (CLTV) is likely very low due to the near absence of repeat purchases and associated revenue from retained customers. This presents the most significant long-term growth challenge.

### 3.5. üöö Operational & Delivery Insights: Fulfillment Efficiency

Delivery performance is a critical aspect of customer satisfaction.

**Key Delivery Metrics:**
*   **On-Time Rate**: 92.10% is commendable, but **8,710 orders (7.90%) were late**.
*   **Average Actual Delivery Days**: 12.01 days.
*   **Average Delay vs. Estimate**: -11.11 days. This indicates that the estimated delivery times are generally conservative, and orders typically arrive much earlier than the maximum estimated window, which can be a positive for customer experience.
*   **Late Delivery Impact**: For the 8,710 late orders, the average delay was 8.73 days, with a maximum recorded delay of **188 days**, which is an extreme outlier and highly detrimental to customer satisfaction.

**Delivery Time Distribution**: The majority of orders (73%) are delivered within 14 days, which is a reasonable performance benchmark.

**Link to Customer Satisfaction**: The negative correlation between delivery days and review scores (highlighted in product insights) underscores the importance of minimizing late deliveries and ensuring efficient logistics. Anomalies in delivery duration directly threaten customer experience.

### 3.6. üö® Anomalies: Unraveling Unusual Patterns

Anomaly detection reveals critical events and recurring issues across different business facets.

*   **Sales Anomalies**:
    *   Confirmed the **Black Friday/Cyber Monday peak in 2017** with significant daily sales spikes on 2017-11-24 ($176,599) and 2017-11-25 ($71,179), and a weekly peak in 2017-W47 ($468,424).
    *   Other notable high sales days include 2018-05-16 ($64,279) and 2018-08-06 ($62,923), which could correspond to other promotional events or seasonal buying patterns.
*   **Order Cancellation Anomalies**:
    *   A concerning number of cancellation anomalies were detected, both daily (33 instances, 9.59%) and weekly (9 instances, 9.38%).
    *   Significant peaks occurred on **2018-08-07 (9 cancellations), 2018-08-09 (6 cancellations)**, and weekly peaks in **2018-W05 (25 cancellations), 2018-W32 (24 cancellations), 2018-W33 (23 cancellations)**. These spikes in cancellations demand immediate investigation into their root causes, such as stockouts, seller issues, or delivery problems.
*   **Successful Orders Anomalies**:
    *   These largely mirrored the sales anomalies, particularly around Black Friday 2017, indicating successful processing of high order volumes during peak times.
*   **Delivery Duration Anomalies**:
    *   Notable spikes in average delivery duration occurred on **2017-01-09 (26.83 days), 2016-10-03 (24.57 days), and 2016-10-04 (24.17 days)**. These periods of significantly longer delivery times likely impacted customer satisfaction and review scores for affected orders.

### 3.7. üìç Regional Insights: Geographic Performance

The marketplace exhibits a highly concentrated geographic footprint.

*   **Dominant Provinces**: The top 5 provinces, led by **SP ($5.77M, 37.41% of total revenue)**, **RJ ($2.05M, 13.33%)**, and **MG ($1.81M, 11.80%)**, collectively account for **73.20% of total revenue**. This indicates a strong market presence in these few key regions.
*   **High Average Customer Value Provinces**: While having fewer customers, provinces like **PB ($273.48 per customer)**, **RO ($246.65 per customer)**, and **AL ($243.40 per customer)** show significantly higher average spending per customer compared to the overall average ($165.13). These regions represent potential growth opportunities for targeted expansion or premium product offerings.

### 3.9. üß© Other Data-Driven Insights: Cross-Dimensional Connections

*   **Single-Purchase Dominance**: The combination of low orders per customer (1.03) and the $0.00 average revenue in cohort analysis strongly indicates that the business model is almost exclusively based on single, transactional purchases. This highlights a fundamental challenge in building customer loyalty and recurring revenue streams.
*   **Delivery as a Churn Factor**: The clear negative correlation between delivery duration and review scores, coupled with detected anomalies in delivery times and cancellation rates, positions delivery performance as a key driver of customer satisfaction and potential churn. Addressing these operational bottlenecks is paramount.
*   **Quality vs. Volume Trade-off**: The fact that some high-revenue products have poor reviews (e.g., 'bebes' product) and top-revenue sellers have mediocre ratings (3.00/5.0) suggests a potential trade-off between maximizing sales volume and maintaining consistent quality/service. This could erode customer trust over time.

## 4. Conclusions & Actionable Recommendations

### Main Insights & Conclusions

The marketplace demonstrates significant operational scale and revenue generation, largely driven by strong performance during peak seasonal events. However, the business faces critical challenges, particularly concerning customer retention, repeat purchase behavior, and the consistency of customer experience. While core regions and categories are performing well, there's a disconnect between top-performing sellers/products by revenue and their corresponding customer satisfaction scores. Operational issues, especially surrounding delivery and order cancellations, significantly impact customer perception. The predominant single-transaction model limits long-term customer lifetime value.

### Proposed Actionable Recommendations

To address these insights and foster sustainable growth, the following recommendations are proposed, prioritized by their potential impact and urgency:

---

### üöÄ Short-Term Actions (Immediate Impact & Operational Improvements)

1.  **Investigate & Resolve Order Cancellation Spikes (High Priority)**
    *   **Action**: Conduct an urgent root cause analysis for the identified daily and weekly cancellation anomalies (e.g., August 2018, February 2018 peaks). Investigate specific products, sellers, inventory levels, or logistical failures associated with these dates.
    *   **Goal**: Reduce cancellation rates and minimize associated revenue loss and customer dissatisfaction.
    *   **Impact**: Direct improvement in customer experience and operational efficiency.

2.  **Address High-Revenue, Low-Review Products (High Priority)**
    *   **Action**: Immediately investigate products like "Product 25c38557..." (bebes) that generate substantial revenue but have critically low review scores (2.68/5.0). Work with the relevant sellers to identify and rectify product quality issues, inaccurate descriptions, or fulfillment problems.
    *   **Goal**: Protect brand reputation and prevent customer churn driven by dissatisfaction with popular products.
    *   **Impact**: Improves individual product performance and overall customer trust.

3.  **Enhance Seller Performance Management (High Priority)**
    *   **Action**: Implement a structured program to support and monitor top-revenue sellers with mediocre ratings (e.g., 3.00/5.0). Provide training, resources, or incentives to improve their customer service, product quality, and delivery speed. For bottom-performing sellers (e.g., <2.75/5.0 rating), establish clear performance improvement plans, or consider off-boarding to protect marketplace quality.
    *   **Goal**: Elevate the overall quality of products and services offered, improving customer satisfaction across the platform.
    *   **Impact**: Strengthens the seller ecosystem and indirectly boosts customer retention.

4.  **Optimize Late Delivery Resolution (Medium Priority)**
    *   **Action**: Analyze the specific causes for the 8,710 late deliveries and the extreme maximum delay of 188 days. Implement robust contingency plans for logistical disruptions, especially during peak seasons (like November), and enhance communication with customers about potential delays.
    *   **Goal**: Minimize delivery delays and improve customer communication, directly impacting review scores and reducing negative sentiment.
    *   **Impact**: Improves customer experience and reduces the negative correlation between delivery time and reviews.

---

### üåü Long-Term Actions (Strategic Growth & Customer Lifetime Value)

1.  **Develop a Comprehensive Customer Retention Strategy (Critical Priority)**
    *   **Action**: Given the critically low customer retention (0.41% by Month 1) and negligible repeat-purchase revenue from cohorts ($0.00 avg revenue), a robust strategy to drive repeat purchases is paramount. This should include:
        *   **Personalized Recommendation Engines**: Leverage purchase history to suggest relevant products.
        *   **Loyalty Programs**: Reward repeat customers with discounts, exclusive access, or points.
        *   **Post-Purchase Engagement**: Targeted email campaigns, feedback requests, and content to keep customers engaged.
        *   **Subscription Models**: Explore viability for suitable product categories.
    *   **Goal**: Transform the business from a transactional to a relationship-based model, significantly increasing Customer Lifetime Value (CLTV).
    *   **Impact**: Sustainable revenue growth, reduced reliance on constant new customer acquisition.

2.  **Strategic Geographic Expansion & Market Penetration (High Priority)**
    *   **Action**: While maintaining focus on dominant provinces (SP, RJ, MG), develop targeted marketing and logistics strategies to increase market share in provinces with high average customer spending but lower customer counts (e.g., PB, RO, AL, PA, PI).
    *   **Goal**: Diversify revenue streams and tap into underserved, high-value customer segments.
    *   **Impact**: Broadens market reach and optimizes revenue per customer.

3.  **Category Portfolio Optimization (Medium Priority)**
    *   **Action**: Conduct a detailed strategic review of underperforming categories (e.g., "seguros_e_servicos", "fashion_roupa_infanto_juvenil"). Determine whether to:
        *   Invest in revitalizing them through new product offerings, marketing, or improved seller onboarding.
        *   De-prioritize resources or consider phasing them out if they consistently fail to meet performance benchmarks.
    *   **Goal**: Optimize resource allocation and focus on categories with the highest growth potential and profitability.
    *   **Impact**: Improved overall business efficiency and profitability.

4.  **Leverage Sales Anomaly Insights for Proactive Planning (Medium Priority)**
    *   **Action**: Use the identified historical sales anomalies (e.g., Black Friday 2017 peaks, May and August 2018 spikes) to forecast demand more accurately for future promotional events. Ensure proactive stock management, seller readiness, and enhanced logistics capacity.
    *   **Goal**: Maximize revenue during peak seasons and prevent operational bottlenecks (like delivery delays or cancellations) that erode customer satisfaction.
    *   **Impact**: Optimized promotional effectiveness and improved customer experience during high-demand periods.
