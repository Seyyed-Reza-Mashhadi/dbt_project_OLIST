üöÄ **3.1. Executive Summary: Key Performance Overview & Strategic Implications**

The business operates with a total revenue of $15.42 million from 96,477 orders and serves 93,357 unique customers through 2,970 sellers, delivering 110,194 items. Key performance indicators reveal an Average Order Value (AOV) of $159.86 and a relatively low Average Basket Size of 1.14 items, suggesting a prevalence of single-item purchases. The Orders per Customer metric stands at 1.03, indicating that a significant portion of customers are one-time purchasers, which points to substantial challenges in customer retention and repeat business. Revenue per Customer averages $165.20, while Revenue per Seller is $5,192.75, with sellers handling an average of 32.48 orders.

Strategically, the business demonstrates a strong capacity for revenue generation, particularly around peak promotional periods, but faces critical challenges in fostering customer loyalty and managing seller performance inconsistencies, especially concerning customer satisfaction and delivery times.

üí∞ **3.2. Financial Performance: Revenue & Growth Trajectory**

The analysis period, spanning 22 months from October 2016 to August 2018, shows an average monthly revenue of approximately $701,020.99 and 4,385 orders. Over the last 12 months, the average monthly revenue increased to $991,834.39, with 6,184 orders, indicating a growing operational scale.

A significant revenue spike was observed in **November 2017**, which recorded the highest month-over-month (MoM) revenue growth of 53.57% ($1,153,528.05) and a concurrent 62.77% increase in orders (7,289). This strong performance was immediately followed by a sharp decline in **December 2017**, where revenue dropped by 26.90%, suggesting a strong reliance on seasonal or promotional events (e.g., Black Friday) followed by a post-peak dip.

Following this, January 2018 saw a strong rebound with 27.92% MoM revenue growth. However, revenue growth then largely plateaued throughout 2018, hovering around $1.0 million to $1.1 million monthly, with slight fluctuations. The latest recorded month, August 2018, experienced a 4.13% MoM revenue decline to $985,414.28, despite a 3.12% increase in orders, indicating a slight decrease in average order value during this period.

üö® **3.3. Anomalies: Identifying Unusual Patterns and Business Impacts**

Anomaly detection highlights several critical events:

*   **Sales Anomalies:** The period around **November 24-25, 2017**, shows significant daily sales peaks ($176,599.36 and $71,179.18, respectively). The corresponding weekly analysis confirms Week 47 of 2017 as an unprecedented peak ($468,424.66). These align perfectly with the overall monthly revenue spike in November, strongly suggesting a successful Black Friday/Cyber Monday event driving exceptional sales volume. Other smaller peaks in **May 2018 ($64,279.82)** and **August 2018 ($62,923.11)** might indicate other successful promotional days or flash sales.
*   **Successful Orders Anomalies:** These mirror the sales anomalies, with November 24, 25, and 27, 2017, registering unusually high numbers of successful orders, further validating the impact of the major promotional event in late 2017.
*   **Order Cancellation Anomalies:** A concerning number of cancellation anomalies were detected, particularly in **August 2018 (Aug 7th with 9 cancellations, Aug 9th with 6 cancellations, and Weekly W32 and W33 with 24 and 23 cancellations respectively)**. Significant cancellation peaks also occurred in **January/February 2018** (Jan 30th, Feb 5th, and Weekly W05, W06) and **October 2016** (Oct 4th, W40). The high frequency of these anomalies (9-10% of daily/weekly data points) suggests recurring underlying issues that warrant immediate investigation, such as stock-outs, payment processing problems, or seller-specific fulfillment issues.
*   **Delivery Duration Anomalies:** Several early dates in the analysis period, specifically **October 2016 (Oct 3rd, 4th)** and **January 2017 (Jan 7th, 9th, 12th)**, exhibited unusually long average delivery times (e.g., 26.83 days on Jan 9, 2017). This indicates early operational inefficiencies or specific logistical challenges during the initial phases of operation. The maximum recorded delay of 188 days for a single order points to extreme outliers that could severely damage customer trust.

üë§ **3.4. Customer Insights: Retention Challenges and Engagement Segments**

The customer base shows a pronounced challenge in retention. The **Cohort Analysis** reveals an extremely low retention rate beyond the initial purchase month: only 0.41% of customers return in Month 1, with rates further declining in subsequent months. This indicates that the business is heavily reliant on new customer acquisition rather than fostering repeat purchases, which is unsustainable for long-term growth. The reported "$0.00 avg revenue" for retained customers in subsequent months within the cohort analysis is highly unusual and suggests a potential misinterpretation or flaw in this specific metric's calculation, as it contradicts the existence of "Loyal Customers" in the RFM analysis.

The **RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) Analysis** for the past 12 months segments the customer base as follows:
*   **Potential Loyalists** form the largest group (48.12% of customers, contributing 57.16% of revenue), indicating a strong base that has purchased recently but might not be highly frequent.
*   **Loyal Customers** (12.12% of customers, 24.53% of revenue) are a valuable segment, contributing a significant portion of revenue despite their smaller size.
*   A substantial **"At Risk"** segment (35.75% of customers, 17.30% of revenue) represents customers who have purchased but are showing signs of disengagement, posing a significant churn risk.
*   The "Lost" segment (3.99% of customers) is relatively small, but the large "At Risk" segment highlights the urgent need for targeted re-engagement strategies.
*   The "Champions" segment is exceedingly small (8 customers, 0.01%), suggesting either that truly exceptional loyalty is rare on the platform or that the RFM model parameters might need adjustment to identify these top-tier customers more effectively.

üöö **3.6. Operational & Delivery Insights: Impact on Customer Satisfaction**

The delivery operations show an On-Time Rate of 92.10%, which is generally good. However, 7.90% of orders (8,710 orders) are delivered late, with an average delay of 8.73 days for these late orders, and a maximum recorded delay of 188 days. The average actual delivery time across all orders is 12.01 days.

The distribution of delivery times shows that while 72.98% of orders are delivered within 14 days, a notable 11.13% of orders take longer than 22 days to reach customers. This long tail of delayed deliveries, particularly the extreme cases, directly links to the observed negative correlation with review scores, reinforcing that delivery performance is a critical driver of customer satisfaction. Issues with delivery delays can erode trust and lead to poor reviews, even for otherwise quality products.

*   **Top Sellers Paradox:** All of the top 5 sellers by revenue (e.g., Seller 4869f7a5...) have an average review rating of 3.00/5.0. This suggests that high sales volume does not necessarily equate to high customer satisfaction for these top performers, indicating a potential trade-off between volume and quality. This could lead to long-term customer churn if not addressed.
*   **Bottom Performers:** Conversely, sellers with consistently low review scores (e.g., Seller 1b1ae47a... at 2.67/5.0) often exhibit slightly longer delivery times, further supporting the link between operational efficiency and customer satisfaction.

*   **Customer Lifecycle and LTV:** The extremely low customer retention rate shown in the cohort analysis is the most critical challenge for the business's long-term viability and Customer Lifetime Value (CLV). The business needs to strategize not only for maximizing these peak periods but also for managing post-peak expectations, inventory, and delivery loads, and mitigating subsequent revenue dips.

**Main Insights & Conclusions:**

The business demonstrates strong revenue-generating capabilities, particularly leveraging seasonal sales events. The market is highly concentrated geographically, with a few product categories and provinces driving most of the revenue, while other smaller regions show promising higher average customer value.
